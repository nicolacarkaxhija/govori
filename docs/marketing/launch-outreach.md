# Launch outreach — committee letter and founding-reviewer invites

Templates for the parallel soft-launches. Replace bracketed placeholders
before sending; links point at the live demo (tunnel or VPS).

## 1. Interslavic committee letter (email, EN)

Subject: An open-source, community-run learning app for Interslavic — built, live, and yours to shape

Dear members of the Interslavic committee,

I am writing to show you something rather than to propose something: a
working, free, open-source learning app for Interslavic — courses,
spaced repetition, a dozen exercise types including morphology drills
built from the community dictionary, dialogues, and stories. It is live
here: [DEMO-URL].

Everything about it is deliberately community-first:

- All learning content is openly licensed (CC BY-SA) with public,
  versioned exports — the community can never lose its own work.
- The dictionary data builds on the community slovnik, with attribution
  on every entry.
- Content changes pass through an in-app review queue: contributions are
  approved by trusted reviewers or by community vote — no gatekeeper,
  no git required.
- The code is AGPL; the platform (an engine called glotty) already
  powers a sister app for Albanian, so Interslavic stands first in a
  family of community languages.

What I would welcome from you, in whatever measure suits you:

1. A look at the app, and your honest judgment.
2. Founding reviewers: 314 drafted sentences and 21 dialogue/story
   scenes await review — in-app, one click per decision. Committee
   members would carry reviewer status from day one.
3. Corrections of course: orthographic or grammatical policy I have
   encoded wrongly, I will fix — the community dictionary is treated as
   the authority.

The app carries a working name (Govori) precisely so that the community
can choose its permanent name before 1.0 — that decision is chartered
to a community vote.

With respect for the work your community has built,
[NAME]
