# Stage 0 — Partner outreach

**Goal:** the Interslavic language community's blessing and participation
before public launch — dictionary partnership confirmed in writing, 2–5
founding Reviewers recruited, and (ideally) a joint announcement plan.

**When:** after the demo slice exists (clickable lessons on real imported
dictionary data, both scripts, offline) — early enough that their input can
still shape the content model, late enough to prove seriousness (ADR 0011).

## The pitch (structure, not script)

1. **What we built and why** — a community-owned learning platform for ISV;
   demo link; open code (AGPL), open content (CC BY-SA), open metrics.
2. **What's theirs** — the dictionary stays theirs (imported under MIT with
   attribution, never forked away); content governance goes to community
   Reviewers; "Besěda" naming offered as a joint decision, never taken.
3. **The disclosure** — AI-drafted seed content exists, is provenance-tagged,
   human-gated, and audited for originality; here is the policy and the
   receipts (ADR 0012/0035).
4. **The ask** — founding Reviewers, feedback on the contrastive-track model,
   and their preferred way to announce it.

## Rules

- First contact in Interslavic where the channel allows it; English fallback.
- Never announce publicly before they've had time to respond privately.
- Every claim in the pitch must be verifiable in the public repo at send time.
